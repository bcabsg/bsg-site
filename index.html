The theme of the BCA Spring Meeting this year was Learning from Others, which highlights the necessity in the current research environment to interact and collaborate to get the best data or to develop a deeper understanding.

The field of Crystallography lends itself to multi-disciplinary research, and the Spring Meeting celebrated this. In addition, we don't only learn from other researchers but from a wide range of stakeholders, no matter if they are from academia, industry or the general public.

To showcase the theme, the subject groups put together an exciting programme with inclusive and collaborative sessions as well as a number of workshops.

BSG sessions included: In-Situ Crystallography, Determining a Protein Crystal Structure in 2025 and Integrative Structural Biology.
